# Canonical Sources — linguistic-historical

Snapshot 2026-04-23.

## Foundational
- **Campbell, L.** (2020). *Historical Linguistics: An Introduction* (4th ed.). Edinburgh / MIT Press. — Canonical comparative-method textbook.
- **Hock, H. H., & Joseph, B. D.** (2019). *Language History, Language Change, and Language Relationship* (3rd ed.). De Gruyter Mouton.
- **Trask, R. L.** (2007). *Historical Linguistics* (2nd ed., revised by Robert McColl Millar). Hodder Education.

## Cognate / comparative tools
- **List, J.-M.** (2014). *Sequence Comparison in Historical Linguistics*. Düsseldorf University Press.
- **List, J.-M., Cysouw, M., Forkel, R.** (2017). *Lexibank, a multilingual lexical database* — bridge between historical and computational linguistics.
- **LingPy**: https://lingpy.org/

## Databases
- **NorthEuraLex** (Dellert et al.): http://northeuralex.org/
- **IE-CoR** (Indo-European Cognate Relationships): https://iecor.clld.org/
- **BDPROTO** (proto-language phoneme inventories): https://bdproto.clld.org/
- **CogNet** (multilingual cognate dataset).
- **Lexibank**: https://lexibank.clld.org/

## Swadesh
- **Swadesh, M.** (1955). *Towards greater accuracy in lexicostatistic dating*. International Journal of American Linguistics.
- Swadesh-list resources curated by SIL and Wiktionary.

## Sound change + computational
- **Bouchard-Côté, A., et al.** (2007). *A Probabilistic Approach to Diachronic Phonology*. EMNLP.
- **Ciobanu, A. M., Dinu, L. P.** (2014). *Automatic detection of cognates*. SIGMORPHON.

## Refresh
- LingPy + Lexibank: continuous (Python releases).
- NorthEuraLex / IE-CoR / BDPROTO: occasional academic releases.
